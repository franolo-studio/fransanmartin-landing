# Product Overview

Personal landing page for **Fran Sanmartín** — a one-page site that introduces Fran and converts visitors into a single action: getting in touch by email.

**Tagline**: _Helping you turn ideas into real, usable tech._

**Pitch**: I help you turn ideas, workflows, or vague product plans into working digital tools. Fast, lean, and user-focused.

## Core Capabilities

- Communicate Fran's value proposition in under 5 seconds (above the fold)
- Provide a single, frictionless contact action (`mailto:` button — no forms, no funnels)
- Offer the same content in **English (default)** and **Spanish**

## Target Use Cases

- A prospect lands from LinkedIn, a referral link, or a business card and wants to know who Fran is and how to contact him
- An existing contact shares the URL as a credibility anchor ("here's the guy I told you about")
- Fran links to it from outbound messages as a soft CTA

## Value Proposition

**For** non-technical founders, PMs, and operators **who** have ideas, workflows, or rough product plans they cannot ship alone, **Fran Sanmartín is** an independent product person **that** turns those ideas into working digital tools — fast, lean, and user-focused — **unlike** agencies (slow, expensive, generic) or freelance developers (build what you ask, not what you need).

## StoryBrand Frame

The page follows a StoryBrand structure (the visitor is the hero, Fran is the guide):

- **Character (you)**: Has an idea or workflow but no clear path from "concept" to "working tool"
- **Problem**: Ideas stay in slides, notebooks, and Notion docs — they never become real
- **Guide (Fran)**: PM with technical depth, ships small things fast, validates with users
- **Plan**: One conversation → small validated build → iterate
- **CTA**: A single button — _Contact me_ (opens email)
- **Failure avoided**: Months of "we should build that someday"
- **Success**: A real, usable tool in users' hands

## Scope Boundaries (what this product is NOT)

- Not a portfolio site (no case study pages, no client logos wall — at least not in v1)
- Not a blog or CMS
- Not a lead-capture funnel (no forms, no newsletter, no calendar embed in v1)
- Not a multi-page site — single page, two locales

---
_v1 is intentionally minimal. Any feature that goes beyond "photo + copy + one CTA in two languages" needs a justification tied to a real user need, not aspiration._
